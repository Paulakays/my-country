Kenya

Kenya is an East African country known for it's stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. It lies along the Indian Ocean and shares borders with Tanzania, Uganda, South Sudan, Ethopia and Somalia. Nairobi is its bustling capital city, while Mombasa serves as a key coastal city.

Key facts about Kenya

  • Wildlife and National Parks: Kenya is famous for its wildlife and natural beauty with iconic national parks such as the Maasai Mara, Amboseli and Tsavo. The Masaai Mara is especially known for the Great Migration, a spectacular event where millions of wildebeest, zebras and other animals migrate between Kenya Tanzania.
  • Mount Kenya: The country is home to Mount Kenya, the second highest mountain in Africa, which is a popular destination for climbers and trekkers.
  • Ethnic Diversity: Kenya has over 40 ethnic groups with the largest being kikuyu, luhya, luo and kalenjin. Swahili and english are the official languages, but many languages are also spoken.
  • Economy: Kenya's economy is the largest in east africa, driven by agriculture, tourism and technology. Nairobi is known as a tech hub, earning the nickname "Silicon Savannah."
  • History and politics: Kenya gained independence from british colonial rule in 1963 and Jomo Kenyatta became the first president. It has since developed into a multi-party democracy though its political landscape has been marked by ethnic tensions and disputed elections.
  • Cultural heritage: Kenya's cultural richness is reflected in it's traditional music, dance and art as well as it's historical sites like Lamu Old Town, a UNESCO world Heritage site, which is one of the oldest swahili settlements in east africa.
  • Sports: Kenya is renowned for it's long distance runners, consistently dominating marathons and olympic competitions with athletes like Eliud Kipchoge setting world records.
Kenya offers a unique blend of natural beauty, wildlife and cultural richness that makes it one of Africa's most visited country.

Counties and regions of Kenya

Kenya is divided into 47 counties, each governed by a county government under the devolved system of governance. The counties are based on the former districts of Kenya and were established under the 2010 constituition to decentralize political and administrative functions. Each county is headed by a governor and has its own county assembly, which legislates on its jurisdiction. This system was designed to bring government services closer to the people and promote local development. Kenya is traditionally divided into several regions, though these regions do not have administrative functions under the current system of counties.
